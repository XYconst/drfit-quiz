PART 3: WHY THE QUESTIONS ARE THE WAY THEY ARE
================================================================================

The Dr.Fit quiz is shorter than BetterMe (27 vs 35 to 50 steps), more medical
than fitness in framing, and personalized via 5 avatar archetypes rather than
generic copy swaps. Each step earns its place by either routing to the avatar
or feeding directly into the personalized result page.

Below is a step-by-step justification.

GROUP A: AVATAR ROUTER (steps 1 to 3)
--------------------------------------

These three steps lock the user into one of five archetypes by the end of
step 3. The archetype drives every personalized element on the result page:
hero headline, projected outcome, comparison block, testimonial set,
program angle.

  Avatar 01: Overweight Man (fat loss, AfterBurn emphasis)
  Avatar 02: Overweight Woman (fat loss, женски метаболизъм framing)
  Avatar 03: Skinny-Fat Man (recomp, AfterBurn plus lean gain)
  Avatar 04: Skinny-Fat Woman (lean tone, "стегни се")
  Avatar 05: Skinny Man (anabolic only, no AfterBurn, "хардгейнър")

Step 1 (Gender) feeds the goal options on step 2. Step 2 (Goal) plus step 3
(Body type) deterministically map to one of the five avatars. There is no
"blocked" path: contradictions resolve toward the closest archetype rather
than rejecting the user.

BetterMe asks the same three pieces in the same order but uses the answers
for cosmetic copy swaps only. Their "endomorph" and "ectomorph" labels are
generic, not productized. We branded ours.

GROUP B: AGE AND PROBLEM AREAS (steps 4 to 5)
--------------------------------------

Step 4 (Age) is for cohort-matching ("92% of people LIKE YOU see results in
30 days") and program calibration. The stat interstitial on step 20 reads
back the user's age tier in the social proof.

Step 5 (Problem areas) is what the program targets. Multi-select because
people rarely have one issue. Used in the workout program prescription and
in the result-page copy: "Twe focus on your X and Y."

BetterMe collects target zones too, just much later in their flow (step 15
of 17). We surface it earlier so the rest of the lifestyle questions feel
context-relevant rather than generic.

GROUP C: LIFESTYLE BASELINE (steps 6 to 12)
--------------------------------------

These seven steps are the metabolic-mechanism diagnostic. Each one fuels a
specific personalized bullet on the result page, section 01 ("Why standard
programs do not work for you"):

  Step 6  Activity level     -> sets caloric calibration
  Step 7  Job movement       -> NEAT (non-exercise activity thermogenesis)
  Step 8  Sleep              -> cortisol bullet
  Step 9  Stress             -> cortisol bullet
  Step 10 Water              -> hydration bullet (used in app, not result)
  Step 11 Diet style         -> insulin bullet
  Step 12 Past attempts      -> "what we will do differently" bullet

This is where Dr.Fit diverges most from BetterMe. BetterMe's lifestyle
questions are mostly cosmetic (they exist to pad the funnel). Ours are
load-bearing: each one writes a sentence on the paywall that the user can
read back about their own answer. This is a direct response to the brand
voice ("Не е била волята. Било е биологията.") which only works if we can
point to specific biology factors.

GROUP D: TRUST INTERSTITIAL #1 (step 13)
--------------------------------------

"Над 10 000 души преминаха същия път с Dr.Fit. 98% казват, че проблемът
не беше волята им. Беше планът."

Inserted at the midpoint to break up the run of lifestyle questions and to
plant the brand thesis (it was the plan, not the willpower) before the user
keeps answering. BetterMe has multiple interstitials at this position; we
have one because the funnel is shorter.

GROUP E: METABOLIC DIAGNOSTIC (steps 14 to 19)
--------------------------------------

These six steps are the most clinical-sounding part of the funnel. They
exist because Dr.Fit's brand is "the program that fixes 4 hormones"
(insulin, leptin, ghrelin, cortisol) and these questions are what the
brand uses to claim a hormonal diagnosis without actually making a medical
claim:

  Step 14 Meal timing        -> insulin / leptin
  Step 15 Energy levels      -> cortisol / glycemic profile
  Step 16 Cravings           -> dopamine plus insulin
  Step 17 Bloating           -> gut and digestive marker
  Step 18 Body temperature   -> thyroid signal
  Step 19 Past best loss     -> realistic projection anchor

BetterMe does not have an equivalent block. Their funnel skips straight
from lifestyle to fitness assessment (squats, push-ups). Ours is the
metabolism-and-hormone block, which is the productized brand mechanism.
This is the strongest content moat the brand has and the funnel reflects
it directly.

GROUP F: TRUST INTERSTITIAL #2 (step 20)
--------------------------------------

"92% от хората с твоя профил виждат първи резултати в първите 30 дни."

Reads back the avatar plus age tier matched in earlier steps. The "твоя
профил" phrase makes the social proof feel personal even though the
underlying claim is segment-wide.

GROUP G: COMMITMENT AND CONSTRAINTS (steps 21 to 23)
--------------------------------------

  Step 21 Motivation         -> personalized hero copy variants
  Step 22 Target date        -> projection chart end-point
  Step 23 Blockers           -> dynamic "without plan vs with Dr.Fit"
                                comparison block on the result page

Step 23 is critical: the comparison block on the paywall is generated from
the user's reported blockers and past attempts. It says back to them, in
their own words, what was failing. BetterMe uses a static comparison
table. Ours is dynamic.

GROUP H: NUMBERS AND PROJECTION (steps 24 to 25)
--------------------------------------

  Step 24 Metrics (h, w, tw) -> BMI projection chart
  Step 25 Projection preview -> shows the projected curve before the user
                                even sees the paywall

The projection-preview step is borrowed from BetterMe (they show a curve
right before email gate). The difference is that ours uses the user's own
height plus weight plus target plus the chosen target date. BetterMe shows
a generic curve with cohort data.

GROUP I: CALCULATING SCREEN WITH PAUSE QUESTIONS (step 26)
--------------------------------------

12-second animated loader. Two questions surface as full-screen overlays
during the loader:

  - At 30 percent: "Кога предпочиташ да тренираш?"
  - At 65 percent: "С какво оборудване разполагаш?"

These are not really part of the avatar routing. They are program-config
questions that we do not need until the program is generated. By tucking
them inside the calculating screen, we get the answers without making the
quiz feel longer. BetterMe has a similar trick.

GROUP J: EMAIL GATE (step 27)
--------------------------------------

Headline reads back what the user just earned: "Твоят 90-дневен план е
готов." Phone is optional, email is required. After submit, lead is
written to the API endpoint, Pixel fires, and the user lands on the
personalized /plan paywall.


================================================================================
KEY DIVERGENCES FROM BETTERME
================================================================================

1. Length. 27 vs ~40+ steps. Every step we kept feeds either the avatar
   router or a specific personalized element on the paywall. BetterMe's
   length is the product (funnel-revenue per question). Ours is not.

2. Voice. Bulgarian "Ти" everywhere. Anti-blame ("Не е била волята.
   Било е биологията."). Mechanism reframe (4 hormones, not 4000 calories).

3. Mechanism IP. Three branded methods anchor every step:
     - Carb-Cycling / Въглехидратна ротация
     - Time-Under-Tension / Време под напрежение
     - AfterBurn / Последващо изгаряне
   Plus "the 4 hormones" framing.

4. Offer. The paywall sells a single 49 EUR commitment fee, fully refunded
   on completion plus before/after photos. Not a hidden-trial subscription.
   The funnel is built to qualify motivated buyers for this offer.

5. Personalization is load-bearing. Every lifestyle question writes a
   bullet on the paywall using the user's own answer. The blockers
   question writes the comparison block. The metrics question feeds the
   projection chart. If we removed any of those questions, the paywall
   would visibly degrade.

6. Avatar segmentation. 5 archetypes with separate testimonial pools,
   stat anchors, hero headlines, and program angles. BetterMe has 1
   funnel with cosmetic copy swaps.

7. Local market. "10 000+ българи" social proof, dynamic city detection
   on stat interstitials when running ads, hardcover-via-Econt on the
   book funnel side.
